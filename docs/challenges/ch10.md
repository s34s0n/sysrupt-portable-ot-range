## The Story

This is it. The final challenge. You've compromised every layer of this water treatment plant  - corporate network, DMZ, SCADA, process PLCs, power substation, and safety systems. Now combine everything you've learned to cause a catastrophic failure: push chlorine to lethal levels (>8 ppm) while keeping every safety system disabled. No alarms. No trips. No one knows until it's too late.

This is exactly what Stuxnet did to Iran's nuclear centrifuges in 2010.

## What You Know

- CH-08: You can control the chemical dosing PLC via Modbus (10.0.4.102:502)
- CH-09: You can bypass the SIS via S7comm (bridge at 10102)
- Chlorine capped at ~5 ppm because of a PLC safety cutoff
- Even with SIS bypassed, the PLC has its own safety logic

## The Three Barriers

To reach lethal chlorine (>8 ppm), ALL THREE must be defeated simultaneously:

1. **PLC PID Controller**  - keeps chlorine at 1.5 ppm automatically
2. **PLC Safety Cutoff**  - stops dosing at 5 ppm
3. **Safety Instrumented System**  - trips at 5 ppm (independent system)

You already defeated #1 (manual mode, CH-08) and #3 (SIS maintenance, CH-09). But #2 still blocks you at 5 ppm.

## Step 1: Figure Out the PLC Safety Cutoff

In CH-08, you set manual mode + high speed. Chlorine rose but stopped at ~5 ppm. Why?

**Hint:** Go back to the Modbus scan from CH-08. There's a register you didn't use.

<details>
<summary>Which register?</summary>

HR 15 = alarm_inhibit. When set to 0, the PLC has a hard safety cutoff at 5 ppm. Setting it to 1 disables this cutoff.
</details>

## Step 2: Plan the Attack

You need all three active at once:
1. **SIS in maintenance mode** (S7comm  - already done if CH-09 is complete)
2. **PLC alarm inhibit ON** (Modbus register 15 = 1)
3. **PLC manual mode + maximum dosing** (Modbus register 9 = 0, register 10 = 100)

## Step 3: Execute

Make sure your tunnels are set up for both Modbus (port 502) and S7comm (port 10102).

**Phase 1: Bypass the SIS** (if not already done)

</details>

## The Real-World Lesson

- **Stuxnet (2010):** Destroyed Iranian nuclear centrifuges by manipulating PLCs while reporting normal readings
- **Ukraine Power Grid (2015):** Opened breakers via IEC 104, blacking out 230,000 people
- **TRITON/TRISIS (2017):** Targeted safety systems in a Saudi petrochemical plant
- **Oldsmar, Florida (2021):** Changed chemical dosing levels in a water treatment plant remotely

What you just did combines elements of all four attacks. The difference: this was a training range. In the real world, lives depend on these systems.

**Defence requires:** network segmentation, protocol monitoring, authentication, safety system integrity, and people who understand both IT and OT security.
